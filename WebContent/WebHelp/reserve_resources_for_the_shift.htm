Reserve resources for the shift

Based on the demand raised for shifts, the test manager or test lead can reserve resources who are available for reservation. In some scenarios, due to the need of specific resources, the test manager or test lead can reserve the resource who are either not marked their availability or available for reservation. These resources are later confirmed for the shift by the resource manager (conflicting resources).

 

Reserve resources for the shift

To reserve the resource for the work package, follow these steps:

  1. Click Plan > Test > Work Package. On the Work Packages pane, expand the product > build, and then select the work package.

  2. On the Work Package Execution page, click the Reserve Resources tab.

  3. In the Pick a Date field, select a date. As a result, the Work Package Resource Plan pane displays the resource demand, available resource for reservation, reserved resource, and the resource demand gap to be filled.

  4. Click the Demand link to view the number of resources required.

  5. Click the Available for Reservation link to view the resources available for reservation.

  6. Click the Reserved link to view the reserved resource for the work package.

  7. Click the Gap link. The Reserve Resources for Work Package page is displayed with Booked, Booked or Available, and All tabs.

  8. The Booked tab displays the no. of resources booked and available for the shift, the Booked or Available tab displays the no. of resources who are either booked or available for the shift, and the All tab displays all resources from the resource pools who are available for the product.
    Note: You can reserve resources who are not marked their availability or booked for the shift, but only after the resource manager confirms their availability in the resource conflicts page, these resources are available for the shift.

  9. On the Booked/Booked or Available/All tab, select the resource checkbox to reserve them.

  10. Based on the selected number of resources, the Available for Reservation, Reserved, and Gap fields are updated.

Resolve conflicting resources

To resolve the resource conflicts, follow these steps:

Click Plan > Configure Work Package. On the Work Packages pane, expand the product > build, and then select the work package.

  1. Click Resource Management > Resource Fulfilment. On the Resource Pool pane, expand the test factory laboratory > Resource Pools, and then select the resource pool.

  2. On the Reservation Conflicts tab, select a from and to date, and click the Apply button. The list of conflicting resource reservation details is displayed with the reservation date, shift type, and no of resources.

  3. Click the Conflicting Reserved Resources link. The list of conflicting reserved resources page is displayed.

  4. Select the checkbox before the Resource Pool field, and click the Confirm Reservation(s) button on the top right corner of the list to reserve the resources for the shift.
